About GirlyPops - How it Started

We’re five girls who bonded over blind boxes, pop culture, and music-and turned that energy into a digital media project. GirlyPops began in our Project Management class when we were asked to form a team and create a digital artifact. After a few brainstorming sessions, we landed on a website that celebrates blind-box culture through original characters and a playful brand world. Here you’ll find our OCs, process sketches, and a Y2K-tinted brand system brought to life through clean UI.

About the Process

To create our OCs, each of us started with inspirations-Pinterest boards, favorite artists, films, songs, and Y2K aesthetics that shaped our characters. We compared notes, pulled common themes, and refined moodboards into color palettes and shapes.

Our artist, Nadia Lee, brought our visions to life with chibi sketches for each character, capturing their personalities in cute, expressive forms.

From there, the design/programming crew translated the aesthetic into brand colors, type choices, and layout ideas. Wireframes became page mockups; mockups became interactive prototypes. Throughout, we iterated based on readability, accessibility, and how “fun” the experience felt.

Our Goal

We’re just five friends making stuff we love. Our goal is to share our love for blind boxes, pop culture, and music and have fun building it together. We sketch late at night, swap ideas, and post our process so you can see the messy middle, not just the final. If you’re into this kind of thing, jump in, send us your thoughts, and help us make the next drop even cooler!
